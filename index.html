Cryptography Primitives Acceleration (CPA Tutorial)

Mar 22: Morning @ ASPLOS'26

Current trajectory of AI systems prioritizes computational performance at scale, often relegating data privacy to an afterthought. This approach is unsustainable. The imminent rise of personalized agents, embodied AI, and sensitive applications like healthcare transforms privacy from a mere compliance feature into a fundamental requirement for earning user trust and ensuring market viability. This creates a critical bottleneck for current systems: how do we secure AI of tomorrow using the non-encrypted infrastructure and emerging dedicated accelerators that we have today?

Homomorphic Encryption (HE) and Zero-Knowledge Proofs (ZKP) offer two complementary paradigms that enable computation and verification over encrypted data and models, providing end-to-end confidentiality and integrity on AI systems. These techniques promise two transformative outcomes:

  • (a) Immediate augmentation of existing AI systems with holistic privacy protection without hardware changes.
  • (b) Guidance on the design of future reconfigurable AI systems that can adapt itself to efficiently serve both full-spectrum PPML and standard ML.

However, deploying HE and ZKP in practice faces steep barriers — notably, orders-of-magnitude increases in computation and memory cost, and accuracy loss from polynomial approximations and floating point quantization.

Existing optimizations have spanned three pillars:

  1. Efficient Model Architecture with redesigned AI operators to minimize non-polynomial computation under encryption.
  2. Compilation that lowers high-precision modular cryptographic operators across privacy tiers into low-precision kernels efficient on today’s hardware or dedicated accelerators.
  3. Hardware that reconfigures itself to efficiently process computation of various shapes and flexible memory reorganizations in HE or optimizes arithmetic and memory organization particularly suited to FHE applications.

This tutorial will guide the ASPLOS community through these advances, offering a unified view across the model–compiler–hardware co-design stack for privacy-preserving AI. Through real system demonstrations and open-source examples, we will highlight practical techniques, quantify trade-offs, and chart future research directions toward making cryptographically secure AI both efficient and deployable.

30 min

Keynote: Privacy-Preserving AI Computing

30 min

Introduction to Verifiable Privacy-preserving AI via HE and ZKP

Memory, Computation and Accuracy overhead of Cryptography primitives (HE and ZKP). Overview of existing HE acceleration techniques (Google TPU, AWS Trainium, Meta MTIA).

Model – Encrypt Model with better Performance / less Accuracy Degradation

  • Overview of components for compiling ML models
  • Hands on: Model rearchitecture
  • Hands on: Arithmetization (polynomial approximation, packing/kernel selection)
  • Hands on: Scheme selection, noise analysis, ciphertext management
  • Hands on: Accuracy degradation considerations
  • Hands on: Differences among schemes (CGGI, CKKS/BGV/BFV)
  • Hands on: Life cycle of model compilation using CKKS
  • Hands on: Export to jaxite (CROSS)
Break (30 min)

Compiler – CROSS: Google TPU for Homomorphic Encryption

TPU-specific Memory and Compute Optimizations for HE.

  • Hands on: Encode, Encryption, Decryption, Decode
  • Hands on: HE Kernels (HEAdd, HEMul, HERotation, HERescale, NTT, Keyswitch)

  • Hands on: MNIST inference benchmark
  • Hands on: Similarity search benchmark
